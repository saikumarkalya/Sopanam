About Kuchipudi

Indian Classical Dance

Kuchupidi is a major Indian clasical dance, created around 500 BCE(Before Common Era) or 2,519 years ago. It is an indian dance form that is often used to depict various types of dramas. It got it's name from the city that the art form was born; Kuchipudi. Even though the dance-drama version of the dance is still practiced the more modernised version is seen as a solo dance.

About Sopanam

Our Kuchipudi Academy

The word Sopanam, when derived from the ancient language of Sanksrit, means the first few steps to learning something new, such as dance. Our main goal is to teach everyone the art of Kuchupudi. We want to educate and promote the community of Indian culture through Kuchipudi.

Teacher







Smitha Kalya is the teacher for Sopanam Kuchipudi Academy. She learned Kuchipudi extensively throughout her childhood and has given many performances throughout her life. She is trained with renowned and established guru Sri Sreelatha Pavani .She grew up to pursue a successful career in IT, after 8 years in IT she decided to spend time pursuing her passion of teaching dance.
